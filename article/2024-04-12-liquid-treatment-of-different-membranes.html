2024-04-12 Liquid treatment of different membranes

Created At：2024-05-28 10:13 Updated At：2024-05-30 09:29

Procedure

  • Checked the integrity of the membrane by imaging them under an optical microscope
  • Treatments included water, unbuffered HF 8%, and buffered HF 5%

 

Main Observations

  • Unbuffered HF\ce{HF}HF treatment damaged the lotus membranes; we are not sure if it was from the treatment or from droping the membrane
  • Both the drums and the trampolines survived the Buffered HF\ce{HF}HF treatment.
  • An addtional test on the trampoline showed that it surface the Unbuffered HF\ce{HF}HF treatment as well.
  • Handling the membranes with teflon twezers is very tricky and might cause more frequent damage of membranes than needed
  • Adding and isoprapanol immersion step after the water steps might help with faster drying and prevent creeping of water from the tweezers on the membrane

HF treatment procedure (done by JA)

  • 10 sec immersion in ≈ 10 cm diameter beaker filled with unbuffered ≈8% HF\ce{HF}HF
  • Subsequent 2x immersion in HX2O\ce{H2O}HX2​O in two different beakers of the same size as the HF\ce{HF}HF beaker
  • Holding with the tweezers to dry
  • The membranes fell on the bench twice.
  • The remaining water droplets was sucked by capillary using clean-room tissues

HF treatment procedure of the trampoline (done by JA)

  • Immersed in 5% buffered HF for 10 sec
  • 2 steps of immersion in MilliQ for roughly 10 sec each
  • First visual inspection suggests that the membrane was intact
  • When trying to release the membrane a water droplet creeped and pulled the membrane to the surface of the tweezers
  • That seemed to have damaged the membrane
  • The membrane was upside down in the box
  • It fell twice the table when mounting it on the microscope
